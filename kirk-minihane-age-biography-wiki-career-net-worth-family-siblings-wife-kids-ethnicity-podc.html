    Kirk Minihane causes chaos at Barstool Sports golf tournament over rules dispute

    This story is about a furious altercation that occurred during the Barstool Sports Writer Cup golf tournament, which pits the prominent sports media company’s writers and hosts against each other in a Ryder Cup-style format. Minihane, a podcaster and radio personality who broadcasts The Kirk Show on Barstool Sports, and Francis Ellis, a comedian and writer who also works for Barstool Sports, were both involved in the incident. They were on opposing teams in a scramble match, which means that each player on a team takes a shot, and the team decides which shot to play from.

    The disagreement began after Ellis made a putt for five, but before his teammate, Foster, could try his putt for four, Minihane decided that their team’s turn was done and that they had to take the five. Minihane contended that once Ellis hit his putt, the team had taken the hole in the scramble style. Ellis, Foster, and Minihane then got into a disagreement about whether it was in for four or five points, with Minihane swearing and yelling at them.

    Dave Portnoy, the creator of Barstool Sports, concurred with Ellis and Foster and accused Minihane of attempting to cheat. The event was captured on film and quickly went viral on social media, with many people debating golf rules and whether Minihane was correct or incorrect. To show their support for Minihane, several of his supporters created the hashtag #KirkWasRight.
