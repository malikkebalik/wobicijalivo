    Fivio Foreign Facts

  • In 2011, he started rapping under the name “Lite Fivio”. He changed his name to “Fivio Foreign” in 2013.
  • With his friends, he had formed a music collective under the name 800 Foreign Side.
  • He was signed to Columbia Records in October 2019.
  • Fivio Foreign’s debut mixtape 800 B.C. (with B.C. stylized as Before Corona) was released on April 24, 2020, through Columbia Records. It consists of songs like Drive By, Big Drip, Wetty, Demons & Goblins (ft. Meek Mill), and Issa Vibe.
  • He was featured on Drake‘s song Demons in May 2020 and the song ranked at #34 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, making it Fivio’s first Billboard-charting single.
  • He was friends with late rappers Pop Smoke (murdered on February 19, 2020), King Von (murdered on November 6, 2020), and T-Dott Woo (murdered on February 1, 2022).
  • On April 8, 2022, Fivio Foreign released his debut studio album B.I.B.L.E. (a backronym for Basic Instructions Before Leaving Earth) via Columbia Records and RichFish. Its tracklist includes Hello (ft. Chlöe and KayCyy), Feel My Struggle, World Watching (ft. Lil Tjay and Yung Bleu), Confidence (ft. ASAP Rocky), City of Gods (with Kanye West and Alicia Keys), What’s My Name (with Queen Naija ft. Coi Leray), Slime Them (ft. Lil Yachty), B.I.B.L.E. Talk (ft. DJ Khaled), Love Songs (ft. Ne-Yo), and Can’t Be Us.
  • The album B.I.B.L.E. peaked at #5 on the US Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums (Billboard) chart, at #8 on the Canadian Albums (Billboard) chart, at #9 on the US Billboard 200 chart, and at #65 on the UK Albums (OCC) chart. The album received generally positive reviews and Kyann-Sian Williams of NME compared B.I.B.L.E. to Kanye West’s 10th studio album Donda (2021).
  • In an interview in July 2022, Fivio Foreign revealed that he was signed by Mase into RichFish Records for only $5,000.
