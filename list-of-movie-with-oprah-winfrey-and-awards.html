    Beloved star started her career as a news anchor in the mid-1970s. She debuted in the film industry in 1985 with The Color Purple. The subsequent year, she hosted Saturday Night Live.

    The Oprah Winfrey Show is a talk show. The first episode of the television talk show was broadcast nationwide from September 1986 until 2011.

    Talking about Oprah education and awards, she transferred to Nicolet High School from Lincoln High School in Milwaukee. The author later joined the speech team at East Nashville High School, where she placed second in national dramatic interpretation.

    Concerning her performances and filmmaking in the entertainment industry, she has won 38 awards and received 67 nominations.

    Some include multiple Daytime Emmy Awards, NAACP Image Awards, and many more. Besides, she has been recognized for her political involvement and charity work. In 2013, former President Barack Obama awarded her the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

    10. A Wrinkle In Time

    A Wrinkle in Time is a movie with Oprah Winfrey in which she played the role of Mrs. Which. The movie is adapted from the 1962 novel by Madeleine L'Engle.

    A Wrinkle in Time, a science-fiction fantasy adventure film, was released on February 26, 2018. Some of the lead cast members are Storm Reid, Reese Witherspoon, Mindy Kaling, Levi Miller, and others. The filming locations include Los Angeles, Eureka, and Humboldt County, New Zealand.

    A Wrinkle in Time narrates the story of a young girl who sets off to space with three astral travelers to find her scientist father. Winfrey portrays one of the oldest astral beings in the movie.

    The movie was awarded Muse Creative Awards in 2018. The same year, it won a silver award from Summit Creative Awards. Altogether, it has five awards and 16 nominations to its credit. 

    9.The Water Man

    The Water Man is a movie with Oprah Winfrey as a producer. Winfrey multimedia production company Harpo Films produced the movie.

    The Water Man is a story about a boy, Gunner Boone, searching for a mythic figure known to have magical healing powers. The boy walks into the woods with a grifter to find him for his sick mother.

    The movie has cast members like David Oyelowo, Rosario Dawson, Lonnie Chavis, and Amiah Miller. David Oyelowo made his directorial debut in the film as well.

    The movie premiered at the Toronto International Film Festival in September 2020 and was released in theaters on May 7. It is also available on Netflix.

    The Water Man won the ReFrame Stamp Award for a narrative and animated feature in 2021. Besides, in 2022, the movie was nominated for BlackReel Awards for outstanding independent film and Image Awards.

    8.Selma

    A movie with Oprah Winfrey named Selma features her as Annie Lee Cooper. Winfrey is also one of the producers of the movie.

    Selma is based on the 1965 protest marches for voting rights from Selma to Montgomery. It premiered in November 2014 and was released in theaters in the United States a month later.

    The two-hour, eight-minute movie featured actors like David Oyelowo, Carmen Ejogo, Tom Wilkinson, Giovanni Ribisi, and many others. Oyelowo portrayed Martin Luther King Jr. in the movie.

    Selma is an Oscar-winning movie. It was awarded an Oscar in 2015 for Best Original Song and received a nomination for Best Motion Picture of the Year. The same year, Selma was awarded an AFI Award in the category of Movie of the Year.

    The 2016 Grammy Award-winning movie has won numerous awards and received 91 nominations. Some of them are four-time Golden Globe Award nominees and 2016 Grammy nominees. 

    7. Beloved

    Movie with Oprah Winfrey Beloved is the debut production film of Harpo Films. Beloved is a psychological horror drama film based on a 1987 novel by Toni Morrison.

    Winfrey played the role of Sethe, a former slave residing on the outskirts of Cincinnati with her daughter, Denver. Sethe is visited by the spirit of a mysterious young lady.

    She welcomes Beloved to the family, and Denver is also happy to have someone to pay with. Eventually, the daughter finds out Beloved is her mother's reincarnated daughter.

    The movie revolves around Sethe, Denver, her friend Paul, and her past life. It features actors such as Kimberly Elise, Jude Ciccolella, Danny Glover, Yada Beener, Emil Pinnock, Calen Johnson, and others.

    The movie won Satellite Awards, Image Awards, and Chicago Film Critics Association Awards in 1999. Besides, it was nominated for an Oscar for best costume design. Also, it has won 23 nominations for various awards under different categories.

    6. Before Women Had Wings

    Before Women Had Wings is one of the movie with Oprah Winfrey released in November 1997. The movie's story is based on Connie May Fowler's semi-autobiographical novel of the same name.

    The movie is about the friendship between a young girl named Bird and a kind woman named Zora Williams (Oprah Winfrey). Zora helps Bird cope with her father's suicide and her mother's abusive nature.

    Bird's parents used to fight a lot. Her father beat his wife now and then. Bearing to the limit, Glory Marie (Bird's mother) walked out of the house with the girls to start a new life.

    The three-girl team shifts to Tampa, where Bird and Williams meet for the first time. At first, Glory doesn't like Williams because of her color, and she even warns her to stop meeting the girls.

    Eventually, at the end of the series, Bird and her sister Phoebe leave with Zora. Ellen Barkin, Tina Majorino, and Julia Stiles are the other main cast of the series.

    Before Women Had Wings won two awards: the Satellite Award and the Primetime Emmy Award in 1998. Besides, it has received four nominations, including Golden Globes and Young Artist Awards.

    5. There Are No Children Here

    There Are No Children Here is a movie with Oprah Winfrey that is somehow related to what her childhood was like. She played LaJoe Rivers, a single mother of two boys growing up in another America.

    The actress' childhood was not easy, growing up under the line of poverty with her single mother. In the movie, Winfrey takes care of her children as a single mother.

    LaJoe gives birth at the age of 14 and has eight kids. Her husband is mostly absent from the family and spends his income on alcohol and drugs.

    The story presents the causes of domestic violence and how younger people get into violence and drug dealing. Moreover, the movie emphasizes that children can do better if given proper chances.

    The top actors in the movie are Winfrey, Keith David, Mark Lane, Norman D., and a few others.  The television movie was nominated for the Humanitas Prize in 1994.

    On the other hand, the novel of the same name won awards like the Carl Sandburg Award, the Christopher Award, and the Helen B. Bernstein Award.

    4. The Women Of Brewster Place

    Movie with Oprah Winfrey list includes The Women of Brewster Place. The film was broadcast on ABC on March 20 in 1989.

    The miniseries is based on Gloria Naylor's 1982 novel of the same name.

    Winfrey has the lead role of Mattie Michael in the series. Alongside her, African-American artists like Cicely Tyson, Robin Givens, Mary Alice, Olivia Cole, Moses Gunn, and several others performed in both episodes of the miniseries.

    The Women of Brewster Place is the story of seven strong-willed females struggling against poverty, bigotry, and troublesome men. The drama starts with Mattie leaving her hometown and her parents after she becomes pregnant.

    She ends up moving to Brewster Place after struggles and tragedies. At Brewster Place, she meets women facing their problems. In the end, all the residents come together to knock down the wall to help one of the residents, Lorraine.

    In 1990, The Women of Brewster Place won the GLAAD Media Award for Outstanding TV Mini-Series. The subsequent year, it was awarded the NAACP Image Award.

    Besides, the mini-series has received numerous nominations, including for Image Awards 1989 and Primetime Emmy Awards 1989.

    3.The Color Purple

    A movie with Oprah Winfrey The Color Purple was her debut in which she portrayed the character Sofia. The Color Purple was nominated for 11 Oscars.

    The Color Purple was released in the United States on December 18, 1985, making a box office collection of $142 million and staying in theaters for 21 weeks. Steven Spielberg rose to prominence as one of the potential directors in the showbiz industry.

    The movie is based on the 1982 Pulitzer Prize-winning novel The Color Purple. It is a story about a young African-American girl named Celie Harris who struggles to find her identity after suffering abuse from her father for decades.

    Overall the movie outlines the domestic violence, poverty, racism, and sexism African-American women faced during the early 20th century.

    Whoopi Goldberg played the lead role, while the movie includes artists such as Danny Glover, Margaret Avery, Akosua Busia, Bennet Guillory, and others. The film was shot in Anson County and Union County in North Carolina.

    The 1985 movie won an ASCAP Award in 1987. Except for these, the movie gained numerous accolades, including nominations and awards.

    Blitz Bazawule is all set to release the second film adaptation of the novel in 2023. This time, Winfrey is on the production team. The movie is planned to release on December 20, 2023.

    2.The Hundred- foot Journey

    The Hundred-foot Journey is an American comedy drama movie with Oprah Winfrey. The film was released in August 2014 and made a box office collection of $89.5 million.

    18 Daytime Emmy Awards winner produced the movie with Steven Spielberg and Juliet Blake. It has actors like Manish Dayal, Helen Mirren, Om Puri, Charlotte Le Bon, and others.

    The Hundred-Foot Journey is a story about a battle between two restaurants in a French village. The Kadam family leaves India and settles in France, where they open an Indian restaurant in front of a French restaurant operated by a Michelin star.

    The two restaurants are a hundred feet apart across the street. The movie is available on Netflix, and those who want to enjoy multicultural understanding through humor should watch the movie at least once.

    The comedy-drama movie is based on a 2010 novel of the same name by Canadian-American novelist Richard C. Morias. It was an international bestseller at the time.

    The two-hour, two-minute movie won Heartland Film Awards for "Truly Moving Picture,".

    The director, Lasse Hallstrom, earned the audience award at the 2014 Norwegian International Film Festival. Also, it was nominated for the Golden Globes in 2015.

    1.The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks

    The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks is one of the movie with Oprah Winfrey. The drama television movie was released on April 22, 2017, on the HBO network.

    The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks is a television drama based on a book by Rebecca Skloot. It was shot in Atlanta and Baltimore, and George C. Wolfe is the director.

    It is the real-life story of an African-American woman, Henrietta Lacks, who was diagnosed with cervical cancer.

    She became a pioneer in changing the course of cancer treatment after her cancer cells, HeLa, were used to create the first immortal human cell line in the early 1950s.

    The series had actors like Oprah, Rose Byrne, Renee Elise Goldsberry, Adrianne Lenox, and a few others. Renee Elise portrayed the real-life Henrietta in the series.

    Winfrey is also one of the executive producers of the drama. Viewers praised her performance as Deborah Lacks, Lacks' daughter. She won the NAMIC Vision Awards for best performance in a drama series.

    The series has won numerous accolades, including the 2017 BlackReel Awards for Television for its outstanding writing in a TV movie. Similarly, The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks earned Gracie Awards in the following years.
