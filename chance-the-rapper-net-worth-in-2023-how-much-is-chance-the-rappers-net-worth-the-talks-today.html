    Chance the Rapper Early Life

    Chancellor Jonathan Bennett’s origin can be traced back to the urban sprawl of Chicago, Illinois where he was born on April 16, 1993. His mother, Lisa Bennett, was employed by the Illinois Attorney General, and his father, Ken Williams-Bennett, served as an aide to the illustrious Barack Obama when he was still a senator. During Obama’s initial tenure, Williams-Bennett took up a post in the Department of Labor. As a teenager, Bennett was given the rare opportunity to meet President Obama. Bennett harbored a fascination with Michael Jackson during his childhood, which his parents’ constant playing of Billie Holiday, Sam Cooke, and other records helped to cultivate his early interest in music. The exposure to Kanye West’s hip-hop music through the radio marked the start of Bennett’s love for the genre, which led him to dabble in rapping as early as his sixth-grade years. While studying at Jones College Prep High School, Bennett forged a hip-hop duo with a close friend, and the two collaborated on music during his freshman year.

    Chance the Rapper Career

    Chance the Rapper gained recognition in 2013 with his second mixtape, “Acid Rap,” which was praised by critics and earned him a growing fan base. In 2016, he released his third mixtape, “Coloring Book,” which became the first streaming-only album to win a Grammy Award. Chance has also collaborated with several well-known artists, including Kanye West, Lil Wayne, and Justin Bieber.
